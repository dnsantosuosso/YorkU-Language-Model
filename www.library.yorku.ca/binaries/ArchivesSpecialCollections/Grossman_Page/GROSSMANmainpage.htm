Company Overview

The Danny Grossman Dance Company was established by Danny Grossman in 1975 (incorporated 1978) at Toronto. Grossman's choreography is the basis for the company's repertoire which includes 'National Spirit,' (1976), 'Endangered Species,' (1981), 'Hot house: thriving on a riff,' (1986), and 'Ground Zero,' (1990). Grossman's works are now in the repertoires of the National Ballet of Canada, Les Grand Ballets Canadiens, and the Paris Opera Ballet. The company has performed in major centres in Canada and the United States as well as in Europe, Israel and Asia. The company has also offered dance programs at York University in its Summer Residency Program, where Grossman once served on the faculty of the Dance Department.

The selection of visual records included on this site are listed in chronological order beginning with Tryptych, created in 1979, and ending with four works created for the 1985-1986 season, Ces Plaisirs, Scherzi, Genus and Nobody's Business For a complete chronology of choreography, a chronology of choreography has been provided.

The Company

Archival Collection at York University

The Danny Grossman Dance Company fonds (F0301) contains 5.64m of textual records, 36cm of graphic material and 108 moving image records. This material has been classified into eight different series: Clipping files, Grant application files, Performance files, Minutes, Administration and fundraising 1975-1986, Programs and reviews, Graphic materials and Moving image records.
