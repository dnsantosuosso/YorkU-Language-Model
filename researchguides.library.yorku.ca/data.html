Welcome!

Welcome to York University's subject research guide for data and statistics. The emphasis of this guide is on Canadian sources, but significant foreign/international sources are also highlighted. Please note that this guide doesn't provide an exhaustive list of available resources; if you do not find what you need here, contact a Data Services Librarian. 

Consultation Appointments

Students and faculty requiring specialized assistance with finding published data & statistics can schedule a consultation appointment with a data librarian.

Questions about Data and Statistics

Are you an student or faculty with a general question about data and statistics? Contact us at datalib@yorku.ca 

Quick links

Here are a few quick links to commonly-used sources of microdata, aggregate data & statistics (note that some may require a York login to access):

  • Canadian Census Analyzer Access census profile data at varying levels of geography, plus microdata and postal code conversion data, for census years 1961, 1971, 1981, 1986, 1991, 1996, 2001, 2006, 2011, 2016.
  • CANSIM (CHASS version) Canadian time-series data
  • Data (Statistics Canada) Catalogue of data available from Statistics Canada, including data tables, data profiles, thematic maps, public use microdata files, and data visualizations.
  • Federated Research Data Repository You can use the Federated Research Data Repository (FRDR) to find research datasets originating from researchers affiliated with Canadian institutions
  • ICPSR Inter-university Consortium of Political and Social Research -- archive of largely US social science survey microdata
  • odesi Source of Statistics Canada public-use microdata files, aggregate statistics tables, and a variety of public opinion poll datasets. Also includes metadata for ICPSR and Statistics Canada master files.
  • Open Data - City of Toronto Official source for Toronto open data from City divisions and agencies. Explore datasets through data visualizations, data stories, blog articles and more.
  • Open Data Portal - Government of Canada Data about Government of Canada services, financials, national demographic information, high resolution maps, and numerous other topics.
  • Scholars GeoPortal Geospatial discovery tool that provides access to large scale geospatial datasets about Ontario and Canada that can be used for mapping or analysis. These datasets encompass both vector and raster data on a variety of topics such as land-use, transportation networks, census geography, aerial imagery, geology, and more.
  • SimplyAnalytics Create and download thematic maps, tables, reports and geospatial data using Canadian and American demographic, business, and marketing variables. It is only available to members of the York University community.Canadian data sources include: Adjusted Census Data, Demographic Estimates, Household Spending, PRIZM, and COVID-19 Outbreak data. American data sources include: U.S. Census Bureau Data, Community Demographic Estimates, Consumer Expenditure Estimates, Uniform Crime Reporting and NOAA Climate Divisional Database.
  • Statista Online portal providing data on the global digital economy, industrial sectors, consumer markets, public opinion, media, demography and macroeconomic trends. Quantitative data from 425 economic sectors in 50 countries (including Canada) are provided with a range of infographic tools for analysis and visualisation.
  • UN Data Gateway to data generated and/or distributed by United Nations statistical agencies
  • World Bank Free and open access to data about development in countries around the globe
