Health Education & Promotion

Health Education & Promotion

As part of SCHW, Health Education & Promotion provides York University students with person-centred, health and well-being support through education, empowerment and access while working towards supplying students with the skills to foster balanced and sustainable choices regarding their health. Our main goal is to create proactive approaches to well-being on campus.

Students can connect with us for a range of services including training programs, workshops and peer-to-peer consulting. We educate in the areas of mental health and well-being, physical health, nutrition, sleep, sexual health, alcohol, cannabis and other drugs.

Programming & Resources

  • Self-Care & Self-Compassion
  • Substance Usage
  • Mental Health & Crisis Support
  • Sleep & Physical Health
  • Sexual Health & Relationships
  • Nutrition

We use mindfulness to tackle the common stress-inducing challenges of university life. We provide you with tools to remain resilient and give yourself compassion during challenging times.

We use a harm-reduction model to educate you on the short- and long-term effects of alcohol, cannabis and other substances. We also share insights on alternative practices to reduce associated risks.

We aim to destigmatize mental health issues by offering information on a wide range of topics, including stress, mental illness, burnout and resources.

Learn about the importance of maintaining your physical health and how related activities such as sleep can positively impact your academics and overall well-being.

Our inclusive harm-reduction and judgement-free approach on a variety of topics associated with sexual health and relationships helps you make informed choices.

We offer guidance on how to incorporate nutritious eating into a student lifestyle, as well as budgeting tips and nutrition fundamentals.

Check out the Feel-good University Eating & Living (FUEL) Cookbook for accessible, balanced recipes and information curated by our team of Peer Health Educators.
