Black Excellence at York University

"We acknowledge that to dismantle the deeply ingrained structures of power and privilege that allow anti-Black racism to thrive, we require a strong, accountable and action-oriented way forward.”

Dr. Sheila Cote-Meek
Vice-President
Equity, People and Culture

Black Excellence at York University (BE YU) provides Black students with the supports they need to transition to university and maximize their student experience on the path to graduation. Through workshops, specialized events, mentoring, academic advising and more, BE YU is designed to champion the academic, professional and personal success of York’s Black student community. 

L’Excellence Noire à l’Université York

« Nous reconnaissons qu’afin de supprimer les structures de pouvoir et de privilège profondément ancrées qui permettent au racisme anti-Noirs de sévir, nous avons besoin d’une approche solide, responsable et concrète pour aller de l’avant. »

Sheila Cote-Meek,
vice-présidente de
l’équité, des personnes et de la culture

L’Excellence Noire à l’Université York (BE YU) fournit aux étudiantes et étudiants noirs les soutiens nécessaires pour une transition réussie et optimise l’expérience étudiante tout au long de leur parcours d’obtention d’un diplôme. BE YU propose notamment des ateliers, des événements, du mentorat et des conseils académiques pour favoriser la réussite académique, professionnelle et personnelle de la population étudiante noire de York.

Develop Resilience and Positive, Race-Based Identities

Student Counselling, Health and Well-being (SCHW) helps you build resilience and realize your full potential while maximizing your journey as a York student. Manage the challenges of university life by accessing supports including walk-in counseling, peer consulting, workshops and more.

Students are welcome to express their preference to meet with counsellors of a particular identity (e.g., Black, male, female, LGBTQ2S+).

Learn More

Développement de résilience et d’identités positives, fondées sur la race

Counseling étudiant, santé et bien-être (SCHW) vous aide à développer votre résilience et à réaliser votre potentiel tout en maximisant votre parcours universitaire. Relevez les défis de la vie universitaire en utilisant des ressources de soutien comme des séances de counseling sans rendez-vous, des conseils de pairs, des ateliers et bien plus encore.

Les étudiantes et étudiants sont invités à exprimer leurs préférences pour rencontrer des conseillers d’une identité particulière (par exemple, personnes noires, hommes, femmes, LGBTQ2S+).

A live phone line is open Monday to Friday from 9a.m. to 4p.m. to support you with general inquiries and to meet with counsellors. Services include the following:

Health Education

  • General health and well-being resources and COVID-19-specific resources
  • Health education and promotion resources and workshops
  • Certificate training programs (e.g. suicide awareness and intervention)
  • Peer-led workshops on physical health, nutrition, sleep, sexual health, alcohol, cannabis, and other drugs, etc.
  • Peer-to-peer consulting
  • Supporting student-led initiatives and grants

Mental Health and Well-Being

  • Crisis/emergency support
  • Walk-in counselling (bilingual)
  • Ongoing counselling/short-term therapy (bilingual)
  • Support groups (e.g., LGBTQ2S+ Support Group, Racialized Students Support Group, Support Group for Survivors of Sexual Violence)
  • 24/7 counselling support for students outside of Ontario via telephone or chat
  • For counselling in French contact counselling@glendon.yorku.ca.

Build Leadership and Create Community

York University’s Student Community and Leadership Development (SCLD) team shares a goal to build and support Black student communities, with programming focused on increasing leadership capacity, connection and social impact on campus.

SCLD provides an abundance of tools, guidance, programs, and workshops to ensure your growth and engagement in student life. You will find everything from first year orientation programming, nutrition, health and wellness to community building and leadership skills.

Academic Accommodations

If you are a student who had an IEP in high school and are looking for guidance or resources, Student Accessibility Services (SAS) offers academic accommodations for continued support in your university studies. You can register with SAS as soon as you accept an offer of admission from the university. Academic Accommodations

Mesures d’adaptation académiques

Student Accessibility Services provides support to:

  • Students with temporary (e.g., concussion) or permanent disabilities (e.g., Depression, Anxiety, ADHD, Diabetes and other medical conditions, mobility issues, vision impairments, Deaf/deafened and hard of hearing and learning disabilities);
  • Students enrolled in full-time or part-time studies at York’s Keele campus;
  • Students registered in undergraduate or graduate studies; and
  • Students enrolled in the School of Continuing Studies.

Student Accessibility Services also provides pre-university/transition programming for prospective and new students to participate in.

Contact Us

Students registered in programs at York’s Glendon Campus should contact Accessibility, Well-Being and Counselling at Glendon regarding supports and services.

Support Black Student Achievement

The transition from high school to university can be financially challenging. Student Financial Services (SFS) is committed to your success and strives to provide the financial assistance you need to meet your academic goals. Through the provision of institutional, government and donor funded financial support, SFS is here to empower you to complete your degree with little or no student debt.

To address the systemic barriers Black students face in post-secondary education, York University has created a special program aimed at increasing financial supports and opportunities available to Black students.

Learn More

Appuyer la réussite des étudiantes et étudiants noirs

La transition de l’école secondaire à l’université peut être difficile du point de vue financier. . Les services financiers aux étudiants (SFS) ont votre réussite à cœur. Ils feront tout leur possible pour vous fournir l’aide financière nécessaire pour atteindre vos objectifs académiques. Grâce à l’aide financière des établissements postsecondaires, à l’aide du gouvernement et aux dons, les SFS sont là pour vous permettre d’obtenir votre diplôme en limitant le plus possible votre emprunt étudiant.

Pour remédier aux obstacles systémiques auxquels la population étudiante noire est confrontée durant les études supérieures, l’Université York a créé un programme spécial visant à accroître les aides financières et les possibilités offertes.

Dedicated financial advisors are available to Black Students, prepared to support you in understanding the many financial aid options available to you, such as:
