We're here to help

 

Being a student comes with many challenges and difficulties. We have many student support systems in place to help! These are a few of them, but you can find more on the Services & Support page. If you're looking for services specific to Glendon campus, please visit the Glendon Current Student website.

  • Academic Advising for guidance on your program/courses
  • Academic Advising Offices listing to help find your faculty's advising office
  • Financial Advising for advice on managing money
  • Career, Education & Development for job and career events, workshops and mentorship.
  • Learning Skills Services provides workshops to help you be a better learner
  • Student Community & Leadership Development to engage in student life and develop leadership skills
  • York Univeristy libraries to help you with your studies
  • York Athletics for a wide variety for sports and recreational activities
  • Academic accommodations with Student Accessibility Services
  • Counselling services for students enrolled at Keele campus
  • Mental health and accessibility for students enrolled at Glendon campus
  • Graduate Students wellness and counselling
  • York University Psychology Clinic for virtual mental health support
  • Atkinson Centre for Mature and and Part-time Students for mature and part-time students
  • Centre for Indigenous Student Services for Indigenous student and community support
  • Office of Student Community Relations crisis support network and advice for students
  • The Centre for sexual violence response and support
  • WalkSAFE mobile app for university safety resources
  • York International for international student support

Land Acknowledgement

We recognize that many Indigenous Nations have long-standing relationships with the territories upon which York campuses are located that precede the establishment of the University. York University acknowledges its presence on the traditional territory of many Indigenous Nations. The area known as Tkaronto has been caretaken by the Anishinabek Nation, the Haudenosaunee Confederacy and the Huron-Wendat. It is now home to many First Nation, Inuit and Métis communities. We acknowledge the current treaty holders, the Mississaugas of the Credit First Nation. This territory is subject of the Dish with One Spoon Wampum Belt Covenant, an agreement to peaceably share and care for the Great Lakes region.
