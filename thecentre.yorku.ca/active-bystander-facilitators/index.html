Active Bystander Facilitators

Sexual Violence Reporting Form

Why did you choose to become an Active Bystander Facilitator?

Anjelica Ramsewack

4th year in the Communications/Marketing Program

I am interested in being an Active Bystander as I believe this is a crucial and important training to ensure that we are able to handle difficult situations effectively. I also want to be able to provide and facilitate more trainings for the Glendon Community.

Arany Navaratnarajah

Specialized Honours BCom - Business Management

To help eradicate rape culture, and tackle the common misconceptions/excuses facilitating sexual harassment. In the wake of Harvey Weinstein’s recent conviction, Tarana Burke reiterated the importance of saying “#MeToo” mentioning that “though today a man has been found guilty, we have to wonder whether anyone will care about the rest of us tomorrow”. Please feel free to reach out to the centre if you’re seeking advice, support, or simply need someone to talk to.

Dael Vasquez

4th year/ Political Science-Communications Double Major

Community building has long been a passion of mine. Thus, it is important to me that students feel safe enough to call York University "home". As an Active Bystander, I aim to uphold our institution's unique values to ensure that members from across both of our campuses feel comfortable in class, in their residences, and throughout our communal spaces.

Emma Verrilli

English

As an active bystander facilitator, I want to create spaces in which students, faculty, and staff are shown that the York community is dedicated to supporting them in whatever capacity they require.

Jaskarn Duhra

4th year, BCOM - Accounting

I want to be an Active Bystander facilitator because I want to further create safer and inclusive spaces, where folks feel protected with their own identities and their lived experiences. Furthermore, using the abilities and knowledge I have been privileged to learn and use it to increase the awareness, educate and empower folks on how we, as not only campus leaders but members of our community, are able to combat Sexual Violence and the ways of intervening and the deescalating of further harm.
Using the privilege that I hold as a male and using it to show folks that although I am not a survivor, Sexual Violence can affect anyone, and isn’t a matter of gender. While also recognizing that conversations about Sexual Violence can be difficult and mentally exhausting, and doing what I can to provide the resources and supports available for folks who are in need of it. We must acknowledge that there is significant work that needs to be done to dismantle rape culture and the systems in place to become survivor-centric through the means of believing and best-supporting survivors of Sexual Violence and the traumas that are faced along with it.

Juvairiyya Hanslod

Political Science and Human Right studies

I am passionate about being an active bystander because it allows each of us to recognize the structural injustices that regularly occur even on our campus. It is crucial to actively dismantle these systemic barriers to create a more equitable, caring and just society. The power of the people can be seen through active engagement and that is why the work of all active bystanders is incredibly important. We all must work to break the silence.

Kien Azinwi

4th year sociology

The work on teaching about consent culture and standing up for and protecting one another is work that could never get redundant or loose it’s importance. I want to be an active bystander not only to help build and empower others to be active bystanders but to remind myself as to why it is message that can’t stop and everyone should learn and aim to be one.

Mark Subekti

MA Humanities

To engage orientation leaders in difficult conversations about navigating the role that they are stepping into. We all have a responsibility to each other, we choose how actively we can uphold this.

Nishani Uthayakumar

4th year, Human Rights and Equity Studies

Within today’s society, violence has become a progressive concern, especially sexual violence. Hence why I believe, in order to achieve awareness and change, it is essential to educate and empower those around us. As an Active Bystander Facilitator, I would like to educate our community members about the importance and increasing need for recognizing sexual violence and consent to cultivate a safe community.

Riaz Nandan

5th year Kinesiology & Health Science

I’m interested in being an active bystander to be as capable as I can for our YFS membership.

Siri Ravipati

Bsc. Specialized Honours Biotechnology

- I chose to be an active bystander facilitator because it is a part of my healing. As cliche as it sounds, my experiences have shaped me into the person I am today despite having witnessed innumerable faulty situations and having been a part of too many. This is one of the most empowering things I have done and it is a privilege to be able to do it again. I met incredible people with similar mindsets, wanting to foster and nurture kindness, and respect in a world that can be so harsh.
I promised myself I would make an active effort and try to make a change, no matter how small. This is something I’m passionate about and I strive to make York and our community a safer space for everyone. I want to make an impact and raise awareness regarding issues like discrimination and sexual violence that are unfortunately, very prevalent in today’s world. I deem it to be my responsibility to reduce/eliminate, educate folks and certainly learn and grow myself.

Sosun Mubbashar

4th year majoring in Human Rights and Equity Studies

This is my second year as an Active Bystander, seeing the impact we had after training was eye opening to me. It made me realize how important it is to teach others about sexual violence in our communities. Being able to get the opportunity again this year to do it for the second time around will allow me to learn more and implement that in our community once again.

Ugonna Kanu

3rd Year/ Human Resources Management

I am interested in becoming an Active Bystander because I feel it is finally time for me to get more involved in the York community. Also, I have always had a passion for mental health, so I believe becoming an Active Bystander will provide me with the opportunity to enlighten the York community about the importance of sexual violence and why such issues should not be belittled anymore in our society.

V Bragagnolo

2nd - Gender, Feminist & Women’s Studies

I am interested in being an Active Bystander because if all of us intervene in every day dangerous events, we can collectively let perpetrators know that their violent behaviour is not acceptable. I believe that taking on a collective responsibility can initiate the deconstruction of societal understandings of violence which have been normalized.

Vikram Vardhan Singh

Calumet College Council President

I want to help people realize their role and recognize the importance of their actions.
